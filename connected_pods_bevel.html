Connected pods — adjusted bevels at the doorway

When two enneahedron modules are joined face-to-face by removing a shared square wall (F8 of module A glued to F0 of module B), the perimeter of the new doorway needs different bevels on the kite panels that border it. Companion to mitre_drawing.html and home_panel_design.html.

4 edges per doorway need re-bevelling — the four perimeter edges of the removed square. They're the L-edges (6.0 m at L = 6 m) on the four kite panels that previously met the now-absent square (F2, F3, F6, F7 in module A, and the matching kites in module B).

All other edges of those kites and all edges of unchanged panels keep their original bevels (33.90° on long edges, 40.89° on short edges).

Why the new bevel is 22.21°

When module B is placed against module A by gluing B's F0 face to A's F8 face, B is the mirror image of A across that plane. So the kite from A and the matching kite from B at any one perimeter edge are mirror twins, joined along an edge that lies in the (now-removed) square's plane.

Each kite originally made dihedral 112.21° with the square it bordered. The angle between the kite's plane and the square's plane is therefore 180° − 112.21° = 67.79°. After the square is removed and B's mirrored kite takes its place, the two kites flare 67.79° on each side of the (former) square's plane. New interior dihedral between the two kites: θ_new = 180° − 2 × (90° − 67.79°) = 180° − 2 × 22.21° = 135.57° New bevel from perpendicular (one side of the joint): β_new = 90° − θ_new / 2 = 90° − 67.79° = 22.21°

Verified by computing the dot product of A's F2 outward normal with B's F2 outward normal (after reflection through the F8 plane): N·N′ = 0.7143, so the angle between normals is acos(0.7143) = 44.43°, giving an interior dihedral of 180° − 44.43° = 135.57°. All four perimeter edges of the doorway give the same answer by symmetry.

Practical notes

  • Mark which kites are "doorway kites" before cutting. They look identical to standard kites until bevelled — easy to mix up. Use a paint dot on the OUTSIDE face of any kite that needs the 22.21° bevel.
  • Same dial-in workflow as before. Set the saw to 22.21° using the Wixey gauge, cut a test offcut, double-check the included angle (should be 2 × 22.21° = 44.43° when two off-cuts are placed face-to-face).
  • Bolted joint detail unchanged. The pine edge laminate, M16 bolts, EPDM gasket, Z-flashing and cover-strip from home_panel_design.html all work identically at the new dihedral. The gasket compresses the same; only the bevel angle of the cut changes.
  • Demountability preserved. The doorway joint is just like any other rim joint — unbolt to separate the modules and move them independently.
  • Sealing. The doorway perimeter is now an interior corner (135.57° looking from inside one module toward the other) rather than a joint between coplanar walls. EPDM bulb gasket along the new edges seals the wall transition; no additional flashing needed unless you're enclosing it for fire separation.
  • For 3+ modules in a row. Each additional module adds another doorway with the same 4 re-bevelled edges. Joining via F0↔F8 (left↔right squares) is the most common — see "Triple Row" or "Family Bungalow (5)" in home_configurations.html.
  • For different join faces. If you join two modules via the bottom (F5↔F5 — two pods stacked) or via kite faces (the various "Kite Pair" presets), the new dihedral is different. Re-derive using the same formula:
    θ_new = 180° − 2·(90° − ε) where ε = 180° − θ_original_with_removed_face
