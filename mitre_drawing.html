Notes

  • The squares (faces F0, F5, F8) are mutually orthogonal — they form the three pairwise-perpendicular "wings" of the solid.
  • The kite is a 41.4°-pointed arrowhead. Its two short edges meet at the wide (≈97.2°) apex; its two long edges meet at the sharp (≈41.4°) apex.
  • Around any vertex of the assembled solid, edges of the same type (S or L) always meet edges of the same type — so the two-bevel scheme is closed under assembly. There is no edge in the polyhedron that needs a third angle.
  • Assembly is forced: the only way to mate panels with edges of equal length is the correct way. No mirrored kites needed.
