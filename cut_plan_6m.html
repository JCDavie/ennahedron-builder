Mark-out routine for the kite (uses only a tape & string)

  1. On a flat sheet, snap a baseline and mark v3 at one end.
  2. From v3, measure L = 6.000 m along the baseline; mark v6.
  3. From v3, swing an arc of radius L = 6.000 m. From v6, swing an arc of radius S = 2.829 m. Their intersection on the working side is v5.
  4. From v3, swing an arc of radius L = 6.000 m. From v5, swing an arc of radius S = 2.829 m. Their intersection (same side) is v2.
  5. Connect v3–v6–v5–v2–v3 with straight lines. Verify diagonals: v3→v5 should be 7.484 m; v2→v6 should be 4.243 m and perpendicular to v3→v5.
  6. Mark face side (writing always on the OUTSIDE face) before cutting bevels.

Practical fabrication notes

  • Bevel direction — every cut leans under the panel (face side wider than back side). When two panels meet, their faces touch; the back sides open out at the dihedral.
  • Mark face side first. Once the bevel is cut, asymmetry of the panel makes the face/back obvious — but it's much cheaper to label up front.
  • One edge, one cut. Both panels meeting at any edge get the same bevel angle (the per-side mitre is half the dihedral, identical on both halves).
  • No mirror parts. The kite is symmetric across its long diagonal; all 6 kites are interchangeable. The 3 squares are interchangeable.
  • Order of cuts. Cut to true shape first (S/L edge lengths and interior angles), then run the bevels; this lets you reference the bevel off the freshly straight edge.
  • Sanity check before glue-up. Any L bevel face placed against any other L bevel face should close the 112.21° dihedral with no gap (use a digital angle gauge against the back-side faces — they should read 112.21°).
  • Material allowance for thickness t. Add 0.673·t on each L-edge and 0.866·t on each S-edge to the stock blank, so the face dimensions are preserved after the bevel runs out the back.
